Honey Badger

Over time, honey badgers have developed a high tolerance to poisons due to their thick skin.

  • Scientific Name: Mellivora capensis.
  • Habitat: Africa and Asia, mainly dry areas.
  • Size: 9 - 11 inches high at shoulder.
  • Diet: Omnivore.

Honey badgers are considered one of the most fearless animals in the world due to their ferocity, toughness and strength. They are part of the weasel family and are related to skunks. They bare the same colors, black with a white stripe on their back, and have the same odor defenses.
